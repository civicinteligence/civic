Emizannyo gifuuse kitundu kya bulamu bwaffe yonna gye
tubeera. Emizannyo egisinze okumanyika mulimu omupiira

ekigwo, jjangu onkwekule, enkuyo, okubaka amayinja,

ebirowoozo, okwogiwaza obwongo, okukolagana obulungi ne
bantu bannaabwe, n’emigaso emirala njolo, naye emigaso gino
giyinza obutasangika mu mizannyo egizannyibwa ensangi zino.

ekidiba wamma ggwe omuzannyo ne gunyuma.
Ng’okukuba eddubi kukutte akati, abakuguse mu kuwuga
baaweekanga bannaabwe abatannayiga ng’olwo boolesa
obukugu n’obumanyirivu bwabwe. Kyokka waliwo bannafe
abaakwatibwanga ensisi ngababaweese, nekyavangamu

1.5: Okuteekateeka / okutegeka omukolo
Omuntu yenna ng’anaakola ekintu ne kimugendera bulungi
ateekwa okuba n’entegeka y’ekyo ky’ayagala okukola. Bye
tukola ne bwe biba by’amu maka gye tubeera. Maama nga
tannafumba kyamisana oba kyaggulo alina okusooka n’amanya
abantu bameka abali ewaka b’agenda okufumbira emmere
ye buli muntu ayinza kulya kyenkana wa? Newankubadde
mu Buganda tugamba nti emmere naddala ey’ekyemisana
tegerwa kuba oyinza okufuna omugenyi essaawa yonna, naye
era maama tayinza kufumba mmere ya bantu makumi ana
ng’ewaka waliwo abantu kkumi bokka. Mu mbeera bwetyo
maama ne bwaba asuubira omugenyi emmere aba agidiibudde,
naye nga kiba kivudde mu butaba na ntegeka.

Uganda is among the top ten countries known for their rich
tourist attractions. Uganda’s variety of natural resources range
from diverse wildlife, forests, mountains, water bodies and
wetlands that support not only wildlife but also bird sanctuaries
and human populations.
A number of Uganda’s people live alongside these tourism
features. They have preserved their cultural diversity as well
as nature herself has, so that Uganda is now popularly known
as a great tourist attraction at the heart of the African continent.

Cholera
Cholera is a water-borne disease which has claimed many
people lives. This disease is caused by using unsafe water
and eating contaminated food. When water gets into contact
with faecal matter, under the ground or when piped water is
exposed to sewage or drainage systems, it gets contaminated.
To prevent contracting and spread of cholera, requires one to
practice good health habits. Boil all drinking water or use water
purifiers to ensure safety of your water. Cook food properly.
Wash vegetables or fruits before eating them raw. Always cover
your food and water. Avoid buying food from road side vendors.
Wash hands with soap before handling food, after visiting a toilet
or latrine. Finally visit a health facility immediately you sense
symptoms like stomach-ache and diarrhoea.
